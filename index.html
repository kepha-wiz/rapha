Our Story

Rapha Orphans International was founded in 2010 with a simple mission: to provide hope and healing to the most vulnerable children in Jinja, Uganda. What started as a small outreach program has grown into a comprehensive support system that addresses education, healthcare, nutrition, and emotional well-being.

Our name "Rapha" comes from the Hebrew word meaning "to heal" - reflecting our commitment to healing not just physical needs, but also the emotional and spiritual wounds that orphaned and vulnerable children often carry.

Today, we operate multiple programs serving hundreds of children and their caregivers, creating sustainable change that ripples through entire communities.

Meet Our Founder

The vision and heart behind Rapha Orphans International

Mukisa Ian

Founder & Executive Director

Born and raised in Jinja, Mukisa Ian witnessed firsthand the challenges faced by orphaned and vulnerable children in his community. After losing his own parents at a young age, he experienced the struggles of growing up without adequate support, which ignited his passion to create a better future for children in similar situations.

With a background in social work and community development, Ian founded Rapha Orphans International in 2010 with just 15 children and a vision to provide holistic care that addresses not just physical needs, but emotional and spiritual healing as well.

"Every child deserves to know they are loved, valued, and have hope for the future. At Rapha, we're not just changing lives—we're transforming generations through the power of love, education, and opportunity."

Under Ian's leadership, Rapha has grown from a small outreach program to a comprehensive organization serving hundreds of children and families. His innovative approach to community development and his unwavering commitment to the children of Jinja has earned him recognition both locally and internationally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our work and how you can help

How does child sponsorship work?

When you sponsor a child for $65/month, you provide comprehensive support including school fees, uniforms, books, healthcare, nutrition, and regular updates on your child's progress. You'll receive letters and photos from your sponsored child, and you can even visit them in Jinja if you wish.

How much of my donation goes directly to programs?

85% of all donations go directly to our programs serving children and families. The remaining 15% covers administrative costs necessary to run our operations effectively. We're committed to transparency and publish our financial reports annually.

Can I visit the children and programs in Jinja?

Yes! We welcome visitors to our center in Jinja. We organize annual trips for sponsors and donors to see our work firsthand and meet the children. Please contact us at least 3 months in advance to arrange a visit.

How do you select the children for your programs?

We work with local community leaders, schools, and churches to identify the most vulnerable children in Jinja. Our selection criteria include orphan status, family income level, health needs, and educational barriers. We prioritize children who have the least access to support systems.

Can I specify how my donation is used?

Yes! You can designate your donation to specific programs like education, healthcare, nutrition, or caregiver empowerment. You can also make a general donation that we'll use where it's needed most at the time.
